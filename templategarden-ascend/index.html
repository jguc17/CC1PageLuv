Our Mission

Our mission is to fundraise for Chinese orphans in a variety of ways– whether this is through funding a child’s education or supporting a foster family with 3-5 children. We seek to aid these children by hosting a variety of fundraisers. We also seek to bring awareness to the current orphan situation in China.

China’s orphan situation is rarely talked about, but it has become a larger problem where there are more than 700,000 orphans, as of 2010, in China. Many of these orphans are abandoned by their parents because of a medical problem which their parents are not able to afford. It is assumed that parents believe leaving their children in the hands of others will give them a better life. However, many times, these orphans can’t afford to receive the surgery that they need and have a hard time being adopted because of the medical problems that they have. China Care’s goal is to be able to help fund these surgeries, so that these children will be able to live healthy lives.

We not only focus on bringing joy to the orphans in China, but we also want to work with the adopted children in our St. Louis community. To do this, we volunteer for the local FCC (Families with Children from China) with our special Dumplings Playgroup events. During these meetings, we mentor adorable adopted Chinese kids and teach them about their Chinese culture in general. More importantly, we serve as positive role models for these children.

iCare Campaign

“iCare. 爱 Care. Do you care? China Care”

iCare campaign is composed of two main aspects of China Care - Fundraising and Advocacy.

Every year, WU China Care strives to fundraise throughout the school year in order to support an orphan’s life-saving medical surgery. In 2013, we successfully raised $2000 for Shi Kang’s congenital heart surgery. In 2014, we raised $5388 for Fang Fang’s spina bifida surgery as well as a year’s support for a foster family. Through many efforts, we hope to successfully reach our goal for this year.benefit dinners, rose sales, and many more! This year, we hope to repeat many of our events from previous years as well as incorporate new events! We hope to be able to meet our goal to sponsor an orphan’s education as well as support a foster family who volunteers to raise 4-5 orphans as their own children.

As well as fundraising for these orphans, we also spend the school year to advocate the cause. Throughout the school year, we plan on showing documentaries about China’s orphans, hosting panels with medical professionals to describe the illnesses and procedures the orphans need, as well as many other events to help spread awareness of the issues China’s orphans face.

Join us on our campaign to help every child find health, family, and love with our iCare Campaign! We even started an online iCare campaign– click here to donate, or simply help us share this page!

Big Sister, Little Sister Mentorship

As kids adopted from China grow older, Wash U China Care wants these kids to be able to develop a life-long tie with not only some college-aged Chinese mentors, but also with at least one other adopted child. “Big Sister, Little Sister” is typically for older children; usually these children have “graduated” from the Dumplings playgroups.

The “Big Sister, Little Sister” program matches a mentor (typically a freshman) with a child and encourages playdates around the St. Louis area. There are two different types of playdates: small group and large group. Small group playdates consist of two to three children and their mentors while large group playdates bring all the participants of the program together. Past activities have included: exploring the City Museum, watching a movie, painting pottery, and baking.

Families interested in participating and student volunteers who are interested in mentoring should e-mail us for more information!

Dumplings Playgroups

Our mission is to bring a slice of Chinese culture to adopted Chinese children across St. Louis. We put on events known as Dumplings Playgroups where Washington University students and St. Louis children interact and work on a variety of activities. Past Dumplings Playgroups have included calligraphy workshops, dumpling-making sessions, and Chinese kite decorations. If playing with and helping kids ages 5-10 is something that sounds appealing to you, we would love to have your help!
The Dumplings playgroups fall on Fridays from 6:30 to 8:30pm, and are usually bimonthly in DUC 276.

Next Dumplings: 4.10.15

Student volunteers who are interested should e-mail us for more information!

Beijing Summer Service Trip

The application for 2015 China Care Summer Service Trip is CLOSED. The trip will be from May 28th to June 13th.

To receive the general information packet for the trip, please contact trip coordinator Niki Song at nikisong@wustl.edu or Joyce Zhou at yusizhou@wustl.edu if you have any questions.

Every year, China Care hosts a summer trip with chosen applicants to the China Care Home in Beijing. There, volunteers spend the day time volunteering in the home interacting with pre- and post- surgery Chinese orphans. Afterwards, they spend the rest of their time learning about Chinese culture and getting a short glimpse of what it’s like living in China.

In previous summers, China Care has sent volunteers for 2 weeks at the home, shadowing doctors at Chinese hospitals, visiting the historical sites of Beijing, and enjoying the vibrant Chinese culture through food and shopping excursions.
